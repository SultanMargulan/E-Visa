How to Obtain a Visa to Europe

“Shengen” refers to a unified visa agreement among 27 European countries that have eliminated border controls among themselves. This allows free movement across most of the EU for short stays. As of 2024, nearly all EU member states are part of it, except for a few non-participating countries and the UK.

A European “multiple-entry” visa, valid for up to 90 days in a 180-day period, can be single-, double-, or multiple-entry. Its maximum validity can reach up to 5 years or just the period of your trip. Tourist, guest (private), and business are common categories.

Generally, you apply at the consulate of the country where you’ll begin and spend most of your trip. Providing a complete, accurate document package is crucial—missing documents may lead to refusal. At the consulate, you may also submit biometric data (fingerprints) and a digital photo, unless you’ve done so within the last 5 years.

IMPORTANT: Citizens of Kazakhstan do require a Shengen Visa.

Common Types of Shengen Visas

Multiple-Entry Visa

A multiple-entry Shengen visa allows repeated entries to Europe within its validity (up to 5 years). However, within each 180-day window, your total stay cannot exceed 90 days. Frequent travelers—those owning property, studying, working, or visiting relatives—are more likely to apply for such a multi-visa.

Tourist Visa

A tourist visa (Type C) is issued to individuals traveling primarily for tourism, sightseeing, or leisure. Along with standard documents, you typically need a travel voucher, hotel booking, or invitation from a tour operator, plus a letter from your workplace confirming your leave if applicable.

Guest (Private) Visa

For visiting family or friends, you’ll need a formal invitation letter, passport details of your host, their proof of financial capability, and documents proving your relationship (if relevant). The rest of the paperwork remains similar to the standard list.

Business Visa

Apart from standard documents, a business visa typically requires an invitation letter from an EU-based company or partner, trade registry excerpts, or other formal proof of a legitimate business relationship.

Required Documents for a Shengen Visa

  • Passport valid at least 6 months past return date
  • Copy of passport ID page
  • 2 color photos (3.5 x 4.5 cm)
  • Completed application form
  • Round-trip flight reservations or proof of travel route
  • Hotel booking or proof of accommodation
  • Bank statements showing sufficient funds
  • International health insurance (coverage of at least €30,000)
  • Additional supporting documents (marriage certificates, prior visa copies, etc.)

Please note, certain groups (minors, pensioners, entrepreneurs, etc.) may require additional documents, such as birth certificates, notarized parental permissions, or company registration forms.

Long-Term (National) Visa D

The term “student Shengen visa” or “working Shengen visa” does not exist in a strict sense. If you are admitted to a European university or have a job offer in the EU, you will need a national visa (Type D) for the specific country. The Shengen agreement covers short stays (Type C), whereas each EU state issues its own long-stay permits.

How Long Does It Take?

Typical processing times vary by country and the applicant’s completeness of documents. With professional assistance, many can finalize within 7–15 days. However, embassies reserve the right to request additional documents, possibly extending processing times.

Visa Costs

Fees differ by category. A tourist, guest, or business Shengen typically starts from 40,000 ₸ + €80 consular fee per passport. National (long-term) visas for study or work often cost more. Children under 6 are often exempt from consular fees; those aged 6–12 may pay 50%. Refunds are usually not issued if your application is denied.
